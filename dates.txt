Don't Text and Drive Scholarship

Deadline: September 30, 2016

Details: The purpose of this scholarship is to help you understand the risks of texting while driving. You will complete a short statement on why you pledge not to text and drive and the winner will receive a $1,000 scholarship.

Raymond Davis Scholarship

Deadline: October 1, 2016

Details: This scholarship was established in memory of Raymond Davis, a nationally recognized and highly-esteemed pioneer in the areas of photographic sensitometry, colorimetry, and microphotography. This scholarship is given to an advanced-level undergraduate or a graduate student with an academic and/or research focus in a field related to imaging

Eligibility: The applicant must be an undergraduate who has completed two academic years of study or a graduate-level student. He or she must be a full-time student at an accredited institution pursuing an academic degree. The acceptance of this scholarship does not preclude the acceptance of additional fellowships, assistant positions, scholarships, or similar awards. The Society may bypass the award if there is no candidate judged suitable by the committee. Applicants do not have to be members of IS&T.


To apply, visit: 
http://www.imaging.org/ist/membership/davis.cfm?AwardCode=RS

ACI Scholarships and Fellowships

Details: American Concrete Institute (ACI) Fellowships are for high-potential undergraduate and graduate students whose studies relate to concrete and are identified by ACI-Member Faculty.

Kelly Engineering Resources Future Engineers Scholarship Program

Deadline: October 17, 2016

Details: Kelly Engineering Resources - an engineering employment provider - offers the Future Engineers Scholarship Program.  This national program will award one outstanding sophomore- or junior-level student with $5000 for 2017 tuition expenses. The winner will be invited to visit a local Kelly Services branch and will be presented with a check payable to his or her current institution. The winner will also receive a commemorative plaque.

Eligibility: Candidates must be full-time students at the time of application submittal, pursuing a degree at an accredited institution of higher education in the United States or Canada during the calendar year, and be accepted into the School of Engineering. Scholarships will not be awarded to 1st Year undergraduate students, or those 4th Year undergraduate students who will be graduating on or before June of the award year. Students must have a minimum of a 3.0 cumulative grade point average (GPA) on a 4.0 scale (or 3.5 on a 4.5 scale, and so forth)


To apply, visit:

Women’s Transportation Seminar (WTS) Washington DC Chapter Scholarship

Deadline: October 17, 2016 by 5:00PM

Details: The mission of WTS is to “transform the transportation industry through the advancement of women.” At WTS-DC, we feel that education is a vital tool to facilitate that advancement, and every year, WTS-DC awards scholarships to deserving young women who are studying transportation at undergraduate and graduate programs in the DC area. As a chapter, we believe that giving scholarships is one of the most important things we do. This year, we anticipate awarding two graduate scholarships of up to $2,000 each, two undergraduate scholarships of up to $2,000 each, and one senior high school scholarship of up to $1,000.

Eligibility: Applicants wishing to be considered by the WTS-DC Chapter must:

·         Be women

·         Have a grade point average of 3.0 or higher

·         Be considering a college degree in transportation or a transportation-related field (high school) or enrolled in a college or university where they are pursuing a degree in transportation or in a transportation-related field, such as transportation planning or engineering, security planning, business management, finance or logistics, or public policy (undergraduate/graduate)

·         Plan to pursue a career in transportation or a transportation-related field

·         Attend school in the Washington, DC metropolitan area
